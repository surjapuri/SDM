Pattern: Fake Courier / Customs Seizure Scam
A caller claims to be from a courier company (FedEx, DHL, India Post) or
customs, stating a parcel addressed to the victim has been seized because
it contains illegal items (drugs, fake passports) or is going to a country
under sanctions. The victim is told to "verify their identity" by
connecting to a police/customs officer on the call, who then transitions
into a digital-arrest-style extortion. Real customs seizures are handled
through official written notices, never resolved by transferring money
over a phone call.
