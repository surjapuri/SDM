Pattern: Digital Arrest Scam
"Digital arrest" is not a real legal process anywhere in India. Scammers
impersonate CBI, police, customs, or narcotics officers over a video or
phone call, claim the victim is linked to a crime (often a parcel with
illegal contents, or money laundering), and tell them they are under
"digital arrest" — meaning they must stay on video call, cannot leave home
or tell anyone, and must transfer money to "clear their name" or avoid a
real arrest. Real Indian law enforcement never conducts arrests, detentions,
or legal proceedings over a video call, and never demands money transfers
to avoid arrest. Victims are often kept on the call for hours under
psychological pressure. If someone describes being on such a call, the
correct advice is: hang up immediately, do not transfer any more money,
and report at cybercrime.gov.in or call 1930.
